Data Defen.se

What are you doing with your data when you die?

Mark your government-issued ID with one of the following licenses to determine what should happen to your data after you die. Break apart a typewriter and use the bits to stamp.

Data-Public

  • Free my data to the public domain / Put my data in the public domain
  • Free to remix with data with that of anyone else
  • Free to algorithmically recreate a version of my former self
  • NB: this license can be augmented by an NC or C amendment to restrict commercial use or make into a free data license.

Erase-Data

  • Erase my data from all public and private sources
  • Send cease and desist letters to any source that continues to host my data
  • Counter data remanence by all possible means, including reformatting or the physical destruction of storage media if necessary

Data-Science

  • Donate my data to science
  • Grant access to accredited researchers only
  • Attribute any insight derived from my data posthumously
  • NB: this license can be augmented by an NC or C amendment to restrict commercial use or make into a free data license.

Data-Family/Friends

  • Grant login credentials to the people specified in Appendix C of the legal code for all my online accounts.
